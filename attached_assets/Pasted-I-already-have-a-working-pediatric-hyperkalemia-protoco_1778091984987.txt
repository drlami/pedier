Scenario-based display:

Scenario 1:
Mild hyperkalemia, asymptomatic, no ECG changes.
Show:

Scenario 2:
Moderate hyperkalemia without ECG changes and clinically stable.
Show:

Show emergency card only:

- Potassium removal therapy.
- ICU/nephrology involvement.
- Repeat ECG and potassium urgently.

Scenario 4:
Suspected pseudohyperkalemia / hemolyzed sample and child is well with no ECG changes.
Show:

- Avoid aggressive treatment unless repeat potassium is high or ECG changes develop.

Scenario 5:
Hyperkalemia with renal failure, oliguria/anuria, or failure to respond to medical therapy.
Show:
- Nephrology urgent consult.
- Consider dialysis according to protocol.
- Continue temporizing measures while arranging definitive potassium removal.
