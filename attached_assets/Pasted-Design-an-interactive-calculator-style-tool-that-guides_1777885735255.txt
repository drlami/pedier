Design an interactive calculator-style tool that guides evaluation and management of children aged 3–36 months presenting with fever and no clear source after history and physical exam.

Base logic on:
- AAP fever without source recommendations (post Hib/PCV era)
- AAP UTI guideline (2011, reaffirmed 2016)
- Modern ER practice (Merck / AAFP review)

IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER:
This tool is for clinical support only. It does not replace physician judgment, local protocols, or antimicrobial resistance patterns.

-------------------------------------
🔷 UI DESIGN
-------------------------------------

Split screen layout:
LEFT SIDE → Input (calculator form)
RIGHT SIDE → Live Result Card

Buttons:
- “Calculate Risk”
- “Reset”
- “Print Protocol”

Color coding:
- GREEN → Low Risk
- YELLOW → Intermediate Risk
- RED → High Risk

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 1: INPUT SECTION
-------------------------------------

Patient Basics:
- Age (months) [3–36 only]
- Weight (kg)
- Temperature (°C)
- Fever duration:
  - <24h
  - 24–48h
  - >48h
  - >5 days

General Appearance:
- Well appearing
- Ill appearing
- Toxic appearing

Immunization:
- Fully immunized (Hib + PCV)
- Incomplete/unknown

Hydration:
- Normal
- Mild dehydration
- Moderate/severe dehydration

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 2: RED FLAGS
-------------------------------------

Checkboxes:
- Toxic appearance
- Poor perfusion / shock
- Lethargy or inconsolable irritability
- Petechial/purpuric rash
- Neck stiffness / bulging fontanelle / seizure
- Persistent vomiting
- Moderate/severe dehydration
- Respiratory distress / hypoxia
- Immunocompromised
- Chronic disease
- Fever ≥40°C
- Fever >5 days
- Cannot ensure follow-up

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 3: UTI RISK CALCULATOR
-------------------------------------

Checkboxes:

Age/Sex:
- Female <24 months
- Male <12 months
- Male <6 months (higher risk)

Circumcision:
- Uncircumcised male <12 months

Fever pattern:
- Fever ≥39°C
- Fever >24–48h
- No identifiable source after exam

Urinary symptoms:
- Dysuria
- Foul-smelling urine
- Frequency/urgency
- Abdominal/suprapubic pain

History:
- Previous UTI

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 4: AUTOMATED LOGIC ENGINE
-------------------------------------

A. HIGH RISK (RED)

If ANY:
- Toxic appearance
- Shock / poor perfusion
- Altered mental status
- Petechial/purpuric rash
- Meningitis signs
- Severe dehydration
- Immunocompromised

→ RESULT = RED (High Risk)

-------------------------------------

B. INTERMEDIATE RISK (YELLOW)

If NOT high risk AND ANY:
- Fever ≥39°C + incomplete immunization
- Fever ≥40°C
- Fever >48h
- Any UTI risk factors
- Ill appearing (not toxic)
- Unreliable follow-up

→ RESULT = YELLOW (Intermediate Risk)

-------------------------------------

C. LOW RISK (GREEN)

ALL must be present:
- Well appearing
- Fully immunized
- Fever <39°C
- No red flags
- Good hydration
- Reliable follow-up

→ RESULT = GREEN (Low Risk)

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 5: UTI DECISION LOGIC
-------------------------------------

If ANY of:
- Female <24 months with fever
- Uncircumcised male <12 months
- Fever ≥39°C + no source
- Fever >24–48h + no source
- Any urinary symptoms
- Previous UTI

→ Show: “UTI SCREENING INDICATED”

-------------------------------------

Urine collection:

Non-toilet trained:
- Catheter or suprapubic aspiration → for culture
- Bag urine → ONLY for screening urinalysis

Toilet-trained:
- Clean catch midstream

-------------------------------------

Interpretation:

If UA shows:
- Leukocyte esterase +
- OR Nitrites +
- OR Pyuria

→ “UTI SUSPECTED”
→ Send urine culture BEFORE antibiotics

-------------------------------------

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 6: LAB RECOMMENDATIONS
-------------------------------------

RED:
- CBC + differential
- Blood culture
- CRP ± procalcitonin
- Urinalysis + culture
- Electrolytes/glucose if needed
- Lumbar puncture if indicated
- Chest X-ray if respiratory signs

-------------------------------------

YELLOW:
- Urinalysis ± urine culture
- Consider CBC, CRP, blood culture
- Chest X-ray ONLY if respiratory signs

-------------------------------------

GREEN:
- No routine labs
- Urine testing ONLY if UTI risk present

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 7: ADMISSION CRITERIA
-------------------------------------

Admit if ANY:
- High risk category
- Toxic appearance
- Shock
- CNS signs
- Severe dehydration
- Persistent vomiting
- Hypoxia
- Suspected serious bacterial infection
- Need IV antibiotics
- Incomplete immunization + abnormal labs
- Poor follow-up

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 8: DISCHARGE CRITERIA
-------------------------------------

ALL must be met:
- Well appearing
- Stable vitals
- No red flags
- Tolerating oral intake
- No sepsis/meningitis concern
- Labs reassuring OR clear diagnosis (e.g. UTI)
- Caregiver reliable
- Follow-up within 24–48h

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 9: ANTIBIOTIC CALCULATOR
-------------------------------------

Show antibiotics ONLY if:
- UTI suspected OR confirmed
- OR bacterial focus identified
- OR high-risk situation

-------------------------------------

For febrile UTI:

Cefixime:
- 8 mg/kg/day once daily

Cephalexin:
- 50–100 mg/kg/day ÷ every 6h

Amoxicillin-clavulanate:
- 40–45 mg/kg/day ÷ every 12h

-------------------------------------

Dose calculation:
Dose = weight × mg/kg/day

Display:
- Total daily dose
- Dose per administration

-------------------------------------

If severe or unable to tolerate oral:

Ceftriaxone:
- 50–75 mg/kg IV/IM

-------------------------------------

WARNING:
“Always send urine culture before antibiotics. Adjust antibiotic choice to local resistance patterns.”

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 10: FINAL RESULT CARD
-------------------------------------

Display:

- Risk category (Green / Yellow / Red)
- Reasons for classification
- Red flags detected
- UTI risk: Yes/No
- Labs required
- Admission vs discharge
- Antibiotic needed: Yes/No
- If yes → show calculated dose
- Safety-net advice
- Follow-up timing

-------------------------------------
🔷 STEP 11: SAFETY NET
-------------------------------------

Return immediately if:
- Lethargy or irritability
- Poor feeding
- Persistent vomiting
- Breathing difficulty
- Rash (especially petechial)
- Seizure
- Dehydration
- Fever >48h after visit or >5 days total
- Caregiver concern

-------------------------------------
🔷 FINAL RULE
-------------------------------------

DO NOT recommend antibiotics automatically for fever.

Antibiotics should ONLY appear if:
- UTI suspected
- Bacterial source found
- High-risk scenario

Otherwise → supportive care only.
